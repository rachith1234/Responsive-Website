Modern
Irrigation

Modern irrigation refers to the use of advanced technology and techniques to optimize the use of water in agriculture. Today's irrigation systems are designed to be efficient, precise, and sustainable, using a range of tools and technologies to deliver water to crops in the most effective way possible.

Micro
Irrigation

Micro-irrigation, also known as trickle irrigation or localized irrigation, delivers water directly to the roots of plants through a network of small tubes or emitters. This approach is highly efficient, reducing water waste and minimizing soil erosion.

Subsurface drip
Irrigation

Subsurface drip irrigation (SDI) involves placing drip tubing below the soil surface, delivering water directly to the root zone of plants. This method reduces water evaporation and soil erosion, resulting in more efficient water use.

Vertical farming
Irrigation

Vertical farming involves growing crops in stacked layers, using hydroponics or other soilless growing methods. Irrigation is typically delivered through a drip system, with nutrient-rich water delivered directly to the roots of the plants. This method is highly efficient, using less water than traditional agriculture and allowing for year-round crop production.
